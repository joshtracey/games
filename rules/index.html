Go
2 players · 9×9, 13×13, or 19×19 board
Play →

Objective

Control more territory than your opponent by surrounding empty intersections with your stones.

Setup

Black goes first. Choose board size (9×9 for beginners, 19×19 for full game) and komi (points added to White's score to offset Black's first-move advantage — 6.5 is standard).

How to play

  • On your turn, place a stone on any empty intersection.
  • Capture: A stone or group with no liberties (adjacent empty points) is removed from the board.
  • Ko rule: You may not recreate the exact board position from the previous move.
  • Suicide: You may not place a stone that would have zero liberties after capture resolution.
  • Pass your turn at any time. Two consecutive passes end the game.

Scoring

Area scoring: count your stones on the board plus empty intersections completely surrounded by your stones. White adds komi. Highest score wins.

💡 Beginners: start on 9×9 to learn capture and territory basics before moving to larger boards.

Backgammon
2 players · 15 checkers each · 24 points
Play →

Objective

Be the first to move all 15 of your checkers off the board.

Direction

White moves from point 1 toward point 24 (bearing off top-left). Black moves from point 24 toward point 1 (bearing off bottom-left).

How to play

  • Roll two dice. Move your checkers the shown number of points — one checker per die, or one checker the combined total.
  • Doubles give you four moves of that number.
  • You may not land on a point occupied by 2+ opponent checkers.
  • Blot: Landing on a single opponent checker sends it to the bar.
  • Bar: Any checker on the bar must re-enter the board before other moves. White enters on points 19–24; Black on points 1–6.

Bearing off

Once all your checkers are in your home board (points 1–6 for White, 19–24 for Black), start bearing off. Roll a die — remove a checker from that exact point. If that point is empty, move a checker from a higher point, or bear off the highest occupied point on an overshoot.

💡 The doubling cube is not included — this is a straight race game.

Chess
2 players · 8×8 board · full rules
Play →

Objective

Checkmate your opponent's King — put it under attack with no legal escape.

Pieces

  • King (♔): One square any direction. Cannot move into check.
  • Queen (♕): Any number of squares in any direction.
  • Rook (♖): Any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Bishop (♗): Any number of squares diagonally.
  • Knight (♘): L-shape (2+1 squares). Jumps over pieces.
  • Pawn (♙): Moves forward one square (two from starting row). Captures diagonally.

Special moves

  • Castling: King moves two squares toward a rook; rook jumps to the other side. Neither may have moved, no pieces between them, and the King may not pass through check.
  • En passant: If an opponent pawn advances two squares past your pawn, you may capture it as if it moved only one square. Must be done immediately.
  • Promotion: A pawn reaching the last rank promotes to Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

End of game

Checkmate: King in check with no legal moves — opponent wins. Stalemate: No legal moves but not in check — draw.

Cribbage
2–3 players · scorekeeper · needs a real deck
Open scorekeeper →

What this app does

This is a digital scoreboard only — you play cribbage with a real deck of cards and use this to track pegs to 121.

Quick rules reminder

  • Each player is dealt 6 cards (5 in 3-player); 2 are discarded to the crib.
  • Pegging: Play cards alternately, calling the running total. Score for 15 (2pts), pairs (2pts), runs, and 31 (2pts) or "go" (1pt).
  • Counting hand: Score pairs, 15s, runs, flush (4+ same suit), and nobs (jack matching starter suit).
  • Crib: Dealer scores the crib as an extra hand.
  • Win: First to 121 points.
💡 Common point values: Pair=2, 15=2, Run of 3=3, Flush of 4=4, Nobs=1, Nibs=2.

3XO
2–4 players · 3×3 grid · ring strategy
Play →

Objective

Be the first player to complete one of the three winning patterns.

Setup

Each player has 9 rings: 3 Small, 3 Medium, 3 Large. The board is a 3×3 grid. Each cell can hold one ring of each size, nested concentrically.

How to play

  • On your turn, select a ring size from your tray, then tap a cell to place it.
  • You may only place a size in a cell if that size slot is not already occupied.

Win conditions

  • Same size, same color: Three of your rings of the same size in a row, column, or diagonal.
  • Escalating line: One Small, one Medium, and one Large ring — all yours — across a row, column, or diagonal (any order).
  • Full stack: All three sizes (S+M+L) in a single cell, all placed by you.

Blokus
4 players · 20×20 board · 21 pieces each
Play →

Objective

Place as many of your 21 polyomino pieces on the board as possible. Fewest squares remaining wins (or most squares placed).

How to play

  • Your first piece must cover your starting corner of the board.
  • Each subsequent piece must touch at least one of your own pieces corner-to-corner.
  • Your pieces may never touch your own pieces edge-to-edge — only diagonally.
  • Opponent pieces may touch your pieces on any side.
  • Pieces can be rotated and flipped before placing.

End of game

When no player can place any more pieces, the game ends. Score = number of squares successfully placed. Highest score wins.

💡 The 1-square monomino is your most flexible piece — save it for tight spots late in the game.

Reversi
2 players · 8×8 board
Play →

Objective

Have the most pieces of your color on the board when the game ends.

Setup

Four pieces in the centre: two Black, two White arranged diagonally. Black moves first.

How to play

  • Place a piece on any empty square that flanks at least one straight line of opponent pieces, with your own piece at the other end.
  • All flanked opponent pieces (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) flip to your color.
  • If you have no legal move, your turn is skipped.

End of game

When neither player can move (board full or no legal moves), count pieces. Most pieces wins.

💡 Corners are extremely valuable — a corner piece can never be flipped.

Nine Men's Morris
2 players · 24 points · 9 pieces each
Play →

Objective

Reduce your opponent to 2 pieces, or leave them with no legal moves.

Phase 1 — Placing

Take turns placing one piece per turn on any empty point. Forming a mill (3 in a straight line) lets you remove any one opponent piece. You cannot remove a piece that is part of a mill unless no other pieces are available.

Phase 2 — Moving

Once all pieces are placed, take turns sliding one piece to an adjacent connected empty point. Forming a mill again lets you remove an opponent piece. A mill can be broken and reformed for repeated captures.

Phase 3 — Flying

When a player is reduced to exactly 3 pieces, they may fly — jumping any piece to any empty point on the board.

End of game

A player loses when they are reduced to 2 pieces, or when they have no legal moves on their turn.

💡 The most powerful strategy is to create two mills that share a piece — sliding it back and forth removes an opponent piece every turn.

Settlers Dice
1–4 players · Catan-inspired dice building
Play →

Objective

Be the first player to reach the target Victory Points (default 10) by building structures.

On your turn

  • Roll all 6 resource dice — each face shows a resource: 🪵 Wood, 🧱 Brick, 🌾 Grain, 🐑 Wool, ⛏️ Ore, or ✨ Gold (wild).
  • Keep any dice by tapping them, then use your one free Reroll on the rest.
  • Build by spending the matching resources shown on your dice.

Structures

  • Road (🪵+🧱): No VP. Build 5+ for Longest Road (+2 VP).
  • Settlement (🪵+🧱+🌾+🐑): 1 VP each. Max 5.
  • City (🌾🌾+⛏️⛏️⛏️): 2 VP, replaces a settlement. Max 4.
  • Dev Card (🌾+🐑+⛏️): Draw a random card — Knight, Victory Point, Road Building, Year of Plenty, or Monopoly.

Special awards

  • Longest Road: First player with 5+ roads gets +2 VP. Stolen if another player builds more.
  • Largest Army: First player with 3+ Knight cards gets +2 VP. Stolen if surpassed.
💡 Gold (✨) is a wild resource — it fills in any missing resource when building.
