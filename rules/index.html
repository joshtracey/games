Go
2 players · 9×9, 13×13, or 19×19
Play →

Objective

Control more territory than your opponent by surrounding empty intersections with your stones.

How to play

  • Place a stone on any empty intersection. Black goes first.
  • Capture: A stone or group with no liberties (adjacent empty points) is removed.
  • Ko rule: You may not recreate the exact board position from the previous move.
  • Suicide: You may not place a stone with zero liberties after captures.
  • Pass at any time. Two consecutive passes end the game.

Scoring

Area scoring: stones on board + surrounded empty intersections. White adds komi. Highest score wins.

💡 Start on 9×9 to learn capture and territory basics.

Backgammon
2 players · 15 checkers each
Play →

Objective

Be the first to bear all 15 checkers off the board.

Direction

White moves toward point 24 (bears off top-left). Black moves toward point 1 (bears off bottom-left).

How to play

  • Roll two dice. Move checkers the shown numbers — one per die or combined.
  • Doubles give four moves of that number.
  • You may not land on a point with 2+ opponent checkers.
  • Blot: Landing on a lone opponent checker sends it to the bar.
  • Bar: Bar checkers must re-enter before any other move.

Bearing off

Once all your checkers are in your home board, bear them off by rolling their exact point number or higher.

Chess
2 players · full rules
Play →

Objective

Checkmate your opponent's King — put it in check with no legal escape.

Pieces

  • King: One square any direction. Cannot move into check.
  • Queen: Any number of squares in any direction.
  • Rook: Any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Bishop: Any number of squares diagonally.
  • Knight: L-shape (2+1). Jumps over pieces.
  • Pawn: Moves forward one square (two from start). Captures diagonally.

Special moves

  • Castling: King moves two squares toward a rook; rook jumps over. Neither may have moved, no pieces between, King may not pass through check.
  • En passant: Capture a pawn that advanced two squares past yours, as if it moved one. Must be done immediately.
  • Promotion: Pawn reaching the last rank becomes Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.
💡 Chess pieces are the Cburnett set by Colin M.L. Burnett, CC BY-SA 3.0.

Reversi
2 players · 8×8 board
Play →

Objective

Have the most pieces of your color when the board is full or neither player can move.

How to play

  • Place a piece to flank at least one straight line of opponent pieces, with your own piece at the other end.
  • All flanked opponent pieces flip to your color.
  • If you have no legal move, your turn is skipped.
💡 Corners can never be flipped — they're the most valuable squares on the board.

Hex
2 players · 7×7, 11×11, or 13×13
Play →

Objective

Red connects the top edge to the bottom. Blue connects the left edge to the right. No draws are possible.

How to play

  • Take turns placing one stone on any empty hex.
  • The first player to form an unbroken chain across their two sides wins.
  • Pie rule: After Red's first move, Blue may swap sides instead of playing normally.
💡 The center of the board is extremely powerful — control it early.

Nine Men's Morris
2 players · 9 pieces each
Play →

Phase 1 — Place

Take turns placing one piece on any empty point. Forming a mill (3 in a line) lets you remove any opponent piece not in a mill.

Phase 2 — Move

Slide pieces to adjacent connected points. Forming a mill again lets you remove an opponent piece.

Phase 3 — Fly

When reduced to 3 pieces, that player may jump to any empty point.

Win

Reduce opponent to 2 pieces, or leave them with no legal moves.

💡 A double mill (two mills sharing one piece) lets you remove a piece every single turn.
⊞
Quoridor
2–4 players · 9×9 board
Play →

Objective

Be the first pawn to reach the opposite side of the board.

How to play

  • Move pawn: One step orthogonally. Jump over an adjacent pawn if the path ahead is clear; otherwise jump sideways.
  • Place wall: Walls span 2 cells and block movement. You have 10 walls each (5 in 4-player).
  • Walls may never completely block any player from reaching their goal.
💡 Don't waste walls early — use them to redirect opponents, not just slow them.
▦
Blokus
2–4 players · 20×20 board · 21 pieces each
Play →

Objective

Place as many of your 21 polyomino pieces as possible. Most squares placed wins.

Rules

  • Your first piece must cover your starting corner.
  • Each new piece must touch at least one of your own pieces corner-to-corner.
  • Your pieces may never touch your own pieces edge-to-edge.
  • Pieces can be rotated and flipped. In 2-player, each player controls two colors.
💡 Save your smallest pieces for tight spots late in the game.

3XO
2–4 players · 3×3 grid
Play →

Setup

Each player has 9 rings: 3 Small, 3 Medium, 3 Large. Each cell holds one ring of each size.

Win conditions

  • Same size, same color: Three of your rings of the same size in a row, column, or diagonal.
  • Escalating line: One S, one M, one L ring — all yours — across a line in any order.
  • Full stack: All three sizes in one cell, all placed by you.

Dots & Boxes
2–4 players · 3×3 to 7×7 grid
Play →

How to play

  • Take turns drawing one line between two adjacent dots.
  • Complete the 4th side of a box to claim it — your initial appears inside.
  • Claiming a box earns another turn.

Win

Most boxes claimed when the grid is full.

💡 Avoid completing the third side of a box — it gifts it to your opponent.

Settlers Dice
1–4 players · Catan-inspired
Play →

On your turn

  • Roll 6 resource dice: 🪵 Wood, 🧱 Brick, 🌾 Grain, 🐑 Wool, ⛏️ Ore, ✨ Gold (wild).
  • Keep any dice, then use one free Reroll on the rest.
  • Build using your dice as resources.

Structures

  • Road (🪵+🧱): Longest Road ≥5 = +2 VP.
  • Settlement (🪵+🧱+🌾+🐑): 1 VP each.
  • City (🌾🌾+⛏️⛏️⛏️): 2 VP, replaces a settlement.
  • Dev Card (🌾+🐑+⛏️): Knight, VP, Road Building, Year of Plenty, or Monopoly.
💡 Gold (✨) fills in any missing resource when building.

Cribbage
2–3 players · scoreboard · needs a real deck
Open scorekeeper →

What this app does

Digital scoreboard only — play with a real deck and use this to track pegs to 121.

Quick rules

  • Deal 6 cards each; discard 2 to the crib.
  • Pegging: Play cards calling the running total. Score for 15 (2pts), pairs, runs, and 31 (2pts) or go (1pt).
  • Counting: Score pairs, 15s, runs, flush, and nobs.
  • Win: First to 121.
💡 Pair=2pts, 15=2pts, Run of 3=3pts, Flush of 4=4pts, Nobs=1pt.

Sudoku
Solo · easy / medium / hard
Play →

Objective

Fill the 9×9 grid so every row, column, and 3×3 box contains each digit 1–9 exactly once.

How to play

  • Tap a cell to select it, then tap a number to fill it in.
  • Given (black) numbers are fixed. Your entries appear in blue.
  • Wrong entries are shown in red and count as mistakes. 3 mistakes ends the game.
  • Pencil mode: Jot candidate numbers in small text to help narrow down options.

Difficulty

  • Easy: 36 given clues — straightforward logic chains.
  • Medium: 28 given clues — requires more forward planning.
  • Hard: 22 given clues — needs advanced techniques.
💡 Use pencil marks to track candidates. When a cell has only one candidate left, it must be that number.

24 Game
1–4 players · arithmetic puzzle
Play →

Objective

Use all 4 numbers on the cards exactly once with +, −, ×, ÷, and brackets to make 24.

How to play

  • Tap the card numbers and operator buttons to build your expression, or type it directly.
  • Operator precedence applies (× ÷ before + −) — use brackets as needed.
  • Tap = 24? to check your answer.
  • In Race mode, the first player to solve the current puzzle scores a point.
💡 Stuck? Use the Hint button to get a nudge, or Solution to see one valid answer.
